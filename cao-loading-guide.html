What are "Cargo Aircraft Only" Goods?

CAO

Stands for "Cargo Aircraft Only"

These are substances forbidden on passenger aircraft due to their high-risk nature or quantity, and require special handling and loading procedures on cargo aircraft.

The Golden Rule: Approved Loading Locations

CAO Dangerous Goods are only permitted in approved locations. Load Planners must ensure every CAO shipment is assigned to a location with the required fire safety standards.

1

Class C Cargo Compartment

The primary location, with built-in fire detection and suppression systems.

Exemptions to the Class C Rule: Visual Examples

Certain CAO goods are exempt from requiring a Class C compartment due to their primary hazard type. They must still be loaded on a Cargo Aircraft, but offer more loading flexibility. Below are visual examples of these exempted dangerous goods.
